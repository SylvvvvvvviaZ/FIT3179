Alcohol, Tobacco, and Other Drugs Impacts in Australia

This dashboard explores key trends and insights related to smoking, drug, and alcohol consumption through a series of interactive data visualizations powered by Vega-Lite. Users can engage with the data, uncover patterns, and better understand the factors influencing substance use.

Smoking and Alcohol Consumption Overview

The data highlights regional impacts of alcohol and tobacco in Australia, showing significant alcohol consumption and smoking rates compared to global trends, with broader implications for public health and policy interventions.



Alcohol-Related Deaths

The data on alcohol-induced deaths shows a correlation between alcohol consumption and mortality rates. Age groups over 45 years in Australia show significant increases in alcohol-related deaths, highlighting crucial public health concerns.

Drug-Related Ambulance Calls

The data on drug-related ambulance calls shows an increase in emergency responses due to opioids and amphetamines.

You can interactively select the year to observe trends and the impact on emergency services.

Heatmap of Substance Abuse

The data on substance abuse highlights drug-induced deaths by type from 1997 to 2022, showing patterns over time. This visualization emphasizes the significant impact of substances like opioids and alcohol on public health in Australia.

Donut Chart of Drug Usage

The data on psychosocial risk factors highlights the most common influences for drug-induced deaths from 2019 to 2022. Key factors include unemployment, family disruption, and legal circumstances, revealing important social issues contributing to the rise in drug-related fatalities.
