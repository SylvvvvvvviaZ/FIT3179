Alcohol, Tobacco, and Other Drugs Impacts in Australia

This visualization highlights the smoking rates across OECD countries, focusing on individuals aged 15 and over who smoke daily. The data reveals significant regional disparities, with some countries showing over 30% daily smokers, indicating cultural and policy impacts on smoking prevalence. This comparison aids in understanding global health implications and policy effectiveness.

This visualization examines drug-related ambulance attendances across Australian states, categorized by drug type from 2015 to 2023. Alcohol intoxication is the most frequent cause, with opioids and amphetamines also contributing significantly. The data allows for trend analysis by region and drug type, shedding light on public health impacts and aiding in resource allocation for emergency services.

This dashboard is created using Vega-Lite visualizations. The data is sourced from the Australian Institute of Health and Welfare reports.
